# Biology — Comprehensive Knowledge Base

## 1. Cell Biology

**Cell Theory**: All living organisms are composed of cells. Cell is the basic unit of life. All cells arise from pre-existing cells (Rudolf Virchow).

**Prokaryotic vs Eukaryotic**: Prokaryotes (bacteria, archaea) — no membrane-bound nucleus, no organelles, circular DNA, smaller (1-10μm). Eukaryotes (animals, plants, fungi, protists) — membrane-bound nucleus, organelles, linear chromosomes, larger (10-100μm).

**Cell Organelles**: Nucleus (DNA storage, transcription), Mitochondria (ATP production — cellular respiration, own DNA, double membrane), Endoplasmic Reticulum (Rough ER — protein synthesis with ribosomes, Smooth ER — lipid synthesis, detoxification), Golgi Apparatus (protein modification, packaging, sorting), Lysosomes (digestion, autophagy, pH 4.5-5), Ribosomes (protein synthesis — 70S prokaryotic, 80S eukaryotic), Peroxisomes (oxidation, catalase), Cytoskeleton (microfilaments, intermediate filaments, microtubules — shape, movement).

**Plant-specific**: Cell wall (cellulose), Chloroplasts (photosynthesis, own DNA), Central vacuole (turgor pressure, storage), Plasmodesmata (cell communication).

**Cell Membrane**: Phospholipid bilayer (fluid mosaic model — Singer and Nicolson, 1972). Integral and peripheral proteins. Cholesterol modulates fluidity. Functions: selective permeability, signal transduction, cell recognition.

**Transport**: Passive (no energy) — Diffusion, Osmosis (water through semipermeable membrane), Facilitated diffusion (via channels/carriers). Active (ATP required) — Primary active transport (Na⁺/K⁺ pump — 3Na⁺ out, 2K⁺ in), Secondary active transport (cotransport). Bulk transport — Endocytosis (phagocytosis, pinocytosis, receptor-mediated), Exocytosis.

**Osmosis**: Hypertonic (cell shrinks/crenation in animals, plasmolysis in plants), Hypotonic (cell swells/lysis in animals, turgid in plants), Isotonic (no net movement).

## 2. Molecular Biology and Genetics

**DNA Structure**: Double helix (Watson & Crick, 1953). Nucleotides: phosphate + deoxyribose sugar + nitrogenous base. Bases: Adenine-Thymine (2 H-bonds), Guanine-Cytosine (3 H-bonds). Antiparallel strands (5'→3' and 3'→5'). Chargaff's rules: A=T, G=C.

**DNA Replication**: Semi-conservative (Meselson-Stahl experiment). Origin of replication, replication fork, replication bubble. Helicase (unwinds), Topoisomerase (relieves tension), Primase (RNA primer), DNA Polymerase III (synthesis 5'→3'), DNA Polymerase I (removes primers, fills gaps), Ligase (joins Okazaki fragments). Leading strand (continuous), Lagging strand (discontinuous — Okazaki fragments). Bidirectional.

**Central Dogma**: DNA → (transcription) → mRNA → (translation) → Protein.

**Transcription**: RNA Polymerase reads template strand 3'→5', synthesizes mRNA 5'→3'. Prokaryotes: single RNA polymerase. Eukaryotes: RNA Pol I (rRNA), Pol II (mRNA), Pol III (tRNA). Stages: Initiation (promoter, TATA box, transcription factors), Elongation, Termination. Post-transcriptional modifications (eukaryotes): 5' cap (7-methylguanosine), 3' poly-A tail, splicing (introns removed, exons joined by spliceosome).

**Translation**: mRNA → protein at ribosomes. Codons (3 nucleotides each). Start codon: AUG (methionine). Stop codons: UAA, UAG, UGA. tRNA carries amino acids via anticodon-codon pairing. Stages: Initiation (small subunit + mRNA + initiator tRNA, then large subunit), Elongation (A site, P site, E site — codon recognition, peptide bond, translocation), Termination (release factors at stop codon).

**Genetic Code**: 64 codons, 20 amino acids + 3 stop. Degenerate (multiple codons per amino acid). Universal (same in most organisms). Non-overlapping, comma-free.

**Mutations**: Point mutations: Silent (no amino acid change), Missense (different amino acid), Nonsense (premature stop). Frameshift: Insertion, Deletion. Chromosomal: Deletion, Duplication, Inversion, Translocation.

**Mendelian Genetics**: Law of Segregation (alleles separate during gamete formation). Law of Independent Assortment (genes on different chromosomes assort independently). Dominant/Recessive. Genotype vs Phenotype. Homozygous vs Heterozygous. Punnett square. Test cross (cross with homozygous recessive to determine genotype).

**Beyond Mendel**: Incomplete dominance (intermediate phenotype), Codominance (both alleles expressed — ABO blood groups), Multiple alleles, Polygenic inheritance (multiple genes, continuous variation — height, skin color), Epistasis (one gene affects expression of another), Pleiotropy (one gene affects multiple traits), Sex-linked traits (X-linked — hemophilia, color blindness).

## 3. Cell Division

**Cell Cycle**: Interphase (G1 — growth, S — DNA synthesis, G2 — preparation) → M phase (Mitosis + Cytokinesis). G0 — quiescent phase. Checkpoints: G1/S, G2/M, Metaphase.

**Mitosis**: Prophase (chromosomes condense, spindle forms), Prometaphase (nuclear envelope breaks), Metaphase (chromosomes align at metaphase plate), Anaphase (sister chromatids separate), Telophase (nuclear envelopes reform, chromosomes decondense), Cytokinesis (cytoplasm divides — cleavage furrow in animals, cell plate in plants). Result: 2 genetically identical diploid cells.

**Meiosis**: Two divisions producing 4 haploid gametes. Meiosis I (reductional — homologous pairs separate): Prophase I (crossing over/recombination at chiasmata), Metaphase I (bivalents align — independent assortment), Anaphase I (homologs separate). Meiosis II (equational — like mitosis): sister chromatids separate. Sources of genetic variation: crossing over, independent assortment, random fertilization.

## 4. Evolution

**Darwin's Theory of Natural Selection**: Variation exists in populations. More offspring produced than can survive. Individuals with advantageous traits survive and reproduce more (survival of the fittest). Over time, favorable traits become more common.

**Evidence for Evolution**: Fossil record, Comparative anatomy (homologous structures — same origin, different function; analogous — different origin, same function; vestigial — reduced function), Molecular biology (DNA/protein sequence similarity), Biogeography, Embryology.

**Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium**: Allele frequencies remain constant in absence of: mutation, gene flow, genetic drift, natural selection, non-random mating. p² + 2pq + q² = 1, p + q = 1.

**Speciation**: Allopatric (geographic isolation), Sympatric (same area, ecological/behavioral isolation), Parapatric (adjacent populations), Peripatric (small isolated population). Reproductive isolation: prezygotic (habitat, temporal, behavioral, mechanical, gametic) and postzygotic (hybrid inviability, sterility, breakdown).

**Mechanisms of Evolution**: Natural selection, Mutation, Gene flow (migration), Genetic drift (bottleneck effect, founder effect), Sexual selection.

## 5. Ecology

**Levels of Organization**: Individual → Population → Community → Ecosystem → Biome → Biosphere.

**Energy Flow**: Producers (autotrophs) → Primary consumers (herbivores) → Secondary consumers (carnivores) → Tertiary consumers → Decomposers. 10% rule: only ~10% of energy transfers to next trophic level. Food chains and food webs.

**Biogeochemical Cycles**: Carbon cycle (photosynthesis, respiration, combustion, decomposition), Nitrogen cycle (fixation, nitrification, denitrification, ammonification), Water cycle (evaporation, condensation, precipitation, transpiration), Phosphorus cycle (weathering, uptake, decomposition — no atmospheric phase).

**Ecological Relationships**: Mutualism (+/+), Commensalism (+/0), Parasitism (+/-), Predation (+/-), Competition (-/-).

**Population Ecology**: Exponential growth: dN/dt = rN (J-shaped curve). Logistic growth: dN/dt = rN(K-N)/K (S-shaped curve, K = carrying capacity). Survivorship curves: Type I (low early mortality — humans), Type II (constant — birds), Type III (high early mortality — fish).

## 6. Human Physiology

**Circulatory System**: Heart (4 chambers), Systemic and Pulmonary circulation. Blood: plasma, RBCs (O₂ transport via hemoglobin), WBCs (immune defense), Platelets (clotting). ABO blood groups and Rh factor.

**Respiratory System**: Gas exchange in alveoli. Inhalation (diaphragm contracts, rib cage expands), Exhalation. O₂ binds hemoglobin, CO₂ transported as bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻). Bohr effect: low pH/high CO₂ → hemoglobin releases O₂.

**Digestive System**: Mouth (amylase) → Esophagus (peristalsis) → Stomach (HCl, pepsin, pH 1.5-3.5) → Small intestine (villi/microvilli, bile, pancreatic enzymes — trypsin, lipase, amylase) → Large intestine (water absorption, gut bacteria) → Rectum.

**Nervous System**: CNS (brain, spinal cord), PNS (somatic, autonomic — sympathetic "fight or flight", parasympathetic "rest and digest"). Neurons: dendrites, cell body, axon, synapse. Action potential: resting (-70mV), depolarization (Na⁺ in), repolarization (K⁺ out). Neurotransmitters: acetylcholine, dopamine, serotonin, GABA, glutamate.

**Immune System**: Innate (non-specific — skin, mucous membranes, phagocytes, inflammation, fever). Adaptive (specific — B cells/antibodies for humoral immunity, T cells for cell-mediated immunity). Memory cells for long-term immunity. Vaccines stimulate adaptive immunity.

**Endocrine System**: Hormones as chemical messengers. Hypothalamus-Pituitary axis. Thyroid (T3/T4 — metabolism), Adrenal (cortisol — stress, adrenaline), Pancreas (insulin — lowers blood glucose, glucagon — raises blood glucose), Gonads (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone).

## 7. Photosynthesis and Cellular Respiration

**Photosynthesis**: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂. Light reactions (thylakoid membrane — Photosystem II, electron transport chain, Photosystem I, ATP synthase → ATP + NADPH, water splitting/photolysis). Calvin Cycle (stroma — carbon fixation by RuBisCO, reduction, regeneration of RuBP → G3P).

**Cellular Respiration**: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP. Glycolysis (cytoplasm — glucose → 2 pyruvate, net 2 ATP, 2 NADH). Pyruvate oxidation (mitochondrial matrix — pyruvate → acetyl-CoA + CO₂). Krebs/Citric Acid Cycle (matrix — 2 ATP, 6 NADH, 2 FADH₂ per glucose). Oxidative Phosphorylation (inner mitochondrial membrane — electron transport chain + chemiosmosis → 26-28 ATP). Total yield: ~30-32 ATP per glucose.

**Fermentation**: Anaerobic. Lactic acid fermentation (muscles, bacteria) or Alcoholic fermentation (yeast — ethanol + CO₂). Net 2 ATP only.
